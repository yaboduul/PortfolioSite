Anna Emerson

Sonic Ink (2019-2022) Desktop application, thermal printer. Free download for Windows and Mac. Donations accepted. Sonic Ink is a novel painting application made with MaxMSP. Instead of stylus pressure controlling the size of the digital brush, as is typical, brush diameter is controlled by the amplitude (loudness) of live audio. This feature allows the user to paint with the participation of ambient sound, their own voice, or any noise-maker. By using a factor as unpredictable as ambient noise in a mark-making tool, Sonic Ink encourages an approach to painting that is unconcerned with tight control and preparation. Instead, it facilitates experimentation, present-mindedness, and embracing chance as a collaborator. Anna Emerson is currently completing her final weeks of undergraduate coursework at the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee, where she studies art and philosophy. Emerson is interested in the many ways beings relate to their environments, and how those relations are impacted by existing infrastructures—both physical and digital. See more of Anna’s artwork and writings at emersauce.com Send a note, or request a copy of Sonic Ink to emersauce@gmail.com

Adam White Kathleen Ossers

Between Rituals, 2021-22, time-based media, the videos on display are simultaneous records of my first year on hormone replacement therapy and the final days at my previous home on 5th and Bruce St. in Walker's Point. The apartment has since been demolished, but my memories of the space are potent; they include new routines, gained confidence, and a self exploration that pulses a humming continuum of joy and despair. A late night recording of me in my new home in Riverwest takes its turn in the loop as well. Playing on its own screen is a diaristic love letter to myself written one month prior to starting HRT. I invite the audience to step into a most beautiful space: finite and vulnerable. Adam Kathleen White Ossers Adam White is a time-based media artist residing in Milwaukee, WI. He is the small business owner of docMyArt and works as a Creative Content Manager at MARN. In all his works he is perpetually drawn to memorializing the mundane. @docmyart ckossers.com

Monique foster

Pioneer Visual Jockey, 2021, Max 8 software and Pioneer DJ board, Not for Sale Interaction/Concept Viewers are encouraged to interact with the DJ board. The visuals displayed are audio reactive and will produce patterns, regardless of engagement, as long as there is sound. However, the buttons, slides, and knobs each control different elements of the visual display, similar to that in audio mixing, and viewers are invited to participate in the experience of manipulating and altering the visuals. Description of work Pioneer Visual Jockey is an interactive performance tool that controls audio reactive visuals. Using the Pioneer Disc Jockey 400 controller and a customized Max 8 patch, participants are able to manipulate the visual outcome that is projected on the walls of the room. Pioneer Visual Jockey takes a unique turn from giving users the ability to mix audio and songs, to now giving users the ability to “mix” and manipulate visual displays. Monique Foster Monique is a senior at UWM’s Peck School of the Arts, majoring in sculpture as well as digital fabrication and design. She often combines digital and analog processes in her projects. Monique is drawn to experimenting with new materials and processes, always seeking to try something she has never done before.

Carter Voras

Floating Grounded, 2021. Home Videos 2022 Interactive Installation NFS Home Videos explores the limits of privacy on the internet, and the relationship between consumer and content. The collection of videos shown are scrubbed from YouTube, restricted to old videos with fewer views. These videos, unlike much of the content we might engage in regularly, aren’t necessarily advertising an aesthetic or lifestyle. With this in mind, it can almost feel wrong to engage in this type of content because of how deeply personal it is. Seren Pellett

Floating Grounded, 2021. In this series of paintings, I have layered paint across different mediums; projected video, painting and drawing on plexiglas, acrylic paint on canvas. In 2020, I found myself falling madly in love with creating paint brush strokes that hang in mid-air and can be scaled to any height in virtual reality. These objects needed a new method of display without a headset on, so that they would be more accessible. Since I am inspired by nature and light, I went back to observation so that I could build an image with three mediums in tandem, which is much easier when referencing the natural world than an invented form in virtual reality. In order to eventually depict virtual reality, I am applying an old process of animation called rotoscoping, in which live action footage is traced frame by frame onto glass.

What we do

The works in the show include game design software, painting and sculpture combined with screens and projection, interactive video collage, audio visualization software, and much more! Interactive and multimedia art is fundamentally collaborative since bridging the gap between the physical and the digital involves combining expertise from disparate areas of work and study, so artists working this way benefit greatly from having a community to play and learn with. Audience members can expect to move through invented environments, interact with light and sound in new ways, break their own path on narrative journeys through time, and even see the breaking point between traditional art and something completely new.
