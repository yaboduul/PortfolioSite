What is interactive and multimedia art?

It's fundamentally collaborative since the audience's participation is what makes the art. The same way performance art such as theater and dance require action, interaction, and participation, so do interactive art installations. Since they often bridge the gap between the physical and the digital by combining expertise from disparate areas of work and study, artists working this way benefit greatly from having a community to play and learn with. Audience members can expect to move through invented environments, interact with light and sound in new ways, break their own path on narrative journeys through time, and even see the divide between traditional art and something completely new.

The art works in this show include game design software, painting and sculpture combined with screens and projection, interactive video collage, and audio visualization software.
